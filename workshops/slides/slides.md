# What Is Molecular Typography?

- A physical & chemical model of letters  
- Letters are considered **molecules**, not metaphors  
- Developed in the 1950s  
- Expanded through the 1960s–80s  
- Faded after 1992

# The Seven Typtoms

- **Itom** — vertical stroke  
- **Etom** — horizontal stroke  
- **Ctom** — curve  
- **Vtom** — diagonal  
- **Ktom** — appendage / spur / tail  
- **Jtom** — descending curve  
- **Dottom** — point particle

# Anatomy of Typtoms

Typtoms feature:

- **Faces** or **curves**  
- **Ends** (short sides)  
- **Positive / negative charges**  
- **Polarity** (except Dottom)  
- **Anchor points** where bonding occurs  

::right::

# Charges & Orientation

Charges define:

- Attraction (opposites)  
- Repulsion (same charge)  
- Preferred orientation on the page  

“Upward”, “leftward”, etc. are **molecular directionality terms**.

<!--
NOTE (IT): Ricorda che questo dà un’illusione di rigore scientifico.
-->

---

# Anchor Points

- All typtoms: end anchors  
- Itom, Etom, Vtom: additional face anchors  
- Ctom/Ktom: end-only  
- Jtom: curve-anchors  
- Dottom: no anchors → held by attraction only

<!--
NOTE (IT): Dottom è la particella speciale: non può legarsi davvero, resta in orbita.
-->

---

# Bond Types

1. **Direct** — end → end  
2. **Flush** — end → face  
3. **Overlap**  
4. **Offset**  
5. **Split** (seen in “t” and “f”)  
6. **Attraction-only** (Dottom)

<!--
NOTE (IT): Puoi dire: “I bond sono i legami chimici tipografici”.
-->

# Molecular Notation

Two systems:

- **Equation form:** Vtom + Etom + …  
- **Formula form:** e.g. **A = V₂E**

Used for:

- Letter comparison  
- Word/sentence formulas  
- Typographic chemistry analysis  

<!--
NOTE (IT): Rendi la formula molto seria, tipo una reazione chimica.
-->

# Example: Letter A

Constructed from:

- **2 Vtom** (diagonals)  
- **1 Etom** (crossbar)

Formula: **A = V₂E**

<!--
NOTE (IT): La A è un perfetto esempio di combinazione minima di diagonali.
-->

---

# Example: Letter E

- **1 Itom** (vertical stem)  
- **3 Etom** (horizontal arms)

Formula: **E = IE₃**

<!--
NOTE (IT): Evidenzia l’importanza della repulsione che mantiene parallele le braccia.
-->

---

# Example: Letter K

- **1 Itom**  
- **2 Ktom** (appendages forming the arms)

Formula: **K = IK₂**

<!--
NOTE (IT): Il Ktom dà forma ai bracci: perfetto esempio del suo ruolo strutturale.
-->

---

# Example: Letter J

- **1 Itom**  
- **1 Jtom** (descender curve)  
- **1 Dottom** (the dot, for uppercase J)

Formulas:  
- j = I J  
- J = I J .

<!--
NOTE (IT): Il Dottom è trattenuto solo da attrazione — non forma un vero bond.
-->

# Curved Letters: C

The **C** is a single **Ctom**:

- Polarity makes the curve bend inward  
- Not flexible enough to close the loop

Formula: **C = C**

<!--
NOTE (IT): Caso emblematico di lettera formata da un solo typtom.
-->

---

# Comma (,)

Two possibilities:

1. A solitary **Ktom**  
2. A decaying **Dottom** whose rotation collapses into a tail

Formula: **, = K** or **. → ,**

<!--
NOTE (IT): Puoi raccontare che la virgola è un “Dottom morto”.
-->

---

# The Letter Q

Built from:

- A circular loop = **two Ctom**  
- A tail = **one Ktom** wedged into the gap

Formula: **Q = C₂K**

<!--
NOTE (IT): Spiega che il Ktom può scivolare → diverse varianti della Q.
-->

---

# The Letter G

Formed by:

- **Two Ctom** with different charge configurations  
- One end breaks & collapses inward  
- Forms the inner “hook” of G

Formula: **G = C₂**

NOTE (IT): Momento “collasso molecolare” molto affascinante.

# Isotopes & Typeface Diversity

Typtoms come in many **isotopes**:

- Itom: rounded, slab, tapered  
- Etom: varied thickness or terminals  
- Ctom: softer or sharper curvature  
- Vtom: steeper or gentler diagonals  
- Dottom: round, square, diamond

These account for typeface diversity.

<!--
NOTE (IT): Il concetto chiave: isotopi = stili tipografici.
-->

---

# Origin of Serifs

Two mechanisms:

1. **Clusters of Etom** attracted to ends  
2. **Electrostatic flaring** of typtoms  
   (anchors push material outward → organic serif)

<!--
NOTE (IT): Dì che questo spiega slab vs serif classiche.
-->

---

# Bold & Electric Behavior

Under electric current:

- Some typtoms **swell** → bold  
- Others **vibrate** → metallic or distressed forms

<!--
NOTE (IT): Momento comico: “Il bold non è un peso, è un comportamento elettromolecolare.”
-->

---

# Human Selection & Monoculture

Humans:

- Prefer consistent isotopes  
- Breed letters for readability  
- Reduce typographic biodiversity  
- Create a typographic **monoculture**

<!--
NOTE (IT): Paragonalo alle mele del supermercato: “tutte uguali”.
-->

  - Some shapes fit

- They may behave like **living organisms**  
- Writing may be a form of **containment**  
- Typography becomes **domestication**  
- Typtoms resemble **genetic material**
